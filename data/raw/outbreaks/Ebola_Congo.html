GIDEON - Epidemiology: Ebola in Congo
Ebola in Congo

Seroprevalence surveys:
     14.3% of wild, non-human primates (1985 to 2000) 1
     4% of bats (Epomops franqueti, Hypsignathus monstrosus, Myonycteris torquata, Micropteropus pusillus, Mops condylurus and Rousettus aegyptiacus) in Congo and Gabon (2003 to 2008) 2 3

It has been estimated that 5,500 gorillas died of Ebola in Congo and Gabon during the outbreaks of 2002 to 2003. 4 5

Notable outbreaks:
     2001 to 2002 - An outbreak (124 confirmed cases, 37 confirmed, 97 fatal) was reported during 2001 to March 2002.  Of the 124 cases, 65 (53 fatal) were detected in Gabon and 59 (20 fatal) in neighboring villages of the Republic of the Congo (Cuvette Region and Kelle District). 6 7 8 9 10 11 12
     2002 - An outbreak (11 cases, 10 fatal) was reported in the Mbomo district, Cuvette Ouest region (northwest) and in Gabon and began after two hunters dismembered a dead chimpanzee.  A total of 9 cases were reported in Congo and 2 in Gabon.  Death of gorillas from Ebola was confirmed in the same region during 2002.
     2003 - An outbreak (143 probable cases, 13 confirmed, 128 fatal) was reported in the Kelle and Mbomo. 13   600 to 800 western lowland gorillas (two-thirds of the population) and a number of forest duikers (Cephalophus sp.) died during the outbreak. 14 15 16
     2003 - An outbreak (35 cases, 29 fatal) was reported in districts of Mbomo in the Cuvette Ouest Departement later in the year. 17 18
     2005 - An outbreak (12 cases, 10 fatal) was reported in Etoumbi and Mbomo in Cuvette Ouest Region. 19 20 21
