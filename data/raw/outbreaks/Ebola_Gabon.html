GIDEON - Epidemiology: Ebola in Gabon
Ebola in Gabon

Chronology:
     1994 - Outbreaks (52 cases total, 31 fatal) were reported in Mekouka, Andock and Minkebe. 1 2
     1996 - An outbreak (37 cases - 20 male and 17 female) were reported in Mayibout II Village (150 Km north of Makokou). The suspect source was a chimpanzee. 3
     1996 to 1997 - An outbreak (60 cases, 45 fatal) of presumed Zaire subtype infection were reported. 4 5 6 7 8  44 of these cases (33 fatal) occurred in the Booue area including Makokou; 14 of the cases (10 fatal) in Libreville; and one case (fatal) in Lamareene.  Subsequently, 11 cases of asymptomatic infection associated with the outbreak were reported. 9 10  Infection by Ebola virus without development of symptoms was reported in some cases. 11
     2001 to 2002 - An outbreak (124 confirmed cases, 37 confirmed, 97 fatal) was reported.  Of the 124 cases, 65 (53 fatal)  were detected in Gabon and 59 (20 fatal) in neighboring villages of the Republic of the Congo (Cuvette Region and Kelle District). 12 13 14 15 16 17 18 19 20
     2002 - An outbreak (11 cases, 10 fatal) in the Mbomo district, Cuvette Ouest region (northwest) and in Gabon began after two hunters dismembered a dead chimpanzee.   A total of 9 cases were reported in Congo and 2 in Gabon.  Death of gorillas from Ebola was confirmed in the same region during that year.
    
Seroprevalence surveys:
     6% of persons in Haut-Ogooue (1982 publication) 21
     1.2% of human sera collected during 1981 to 1997 - including 6 samples collected more than 3 years prior to the first known outbreak. 22
     10.2% of persons in Ogooue-Ivindo (northeast) in 1995 23
     1.4% of persons in Ogooue-Ivindo (northeast) in 1997 24
     15.3% of villagers in Gabon (2010 publication) 25
     1.1% of wild, non-human primates (1985 to 2000) 26
     5% of bats (2003 to 2006) 27 28
     4% of bats (Epomops franqueti, Hypsignathus monstrosus, Myonycteris torquata, Micropteropus pusillus, Mops condylurus and Rousettus aegyptiacus) in Congo and Gabon (2003 to 2008) 29 30

It has been estimated that 5,500 gorillas died of Ebola in Congo and Gabon during the outbreaks of 2002 to 2003. 31 32

Asymptomatic infection of dogs in affected areas has been documented. 33
